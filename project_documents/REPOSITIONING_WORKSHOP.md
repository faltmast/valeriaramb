## BLOCK 1: THE CURRENT STATE (10 min)

> Facilitator note: Listen more than talk. Capture exact words — they become copy.

### Questions to ask Valeria:

1. **"Describe your company in one sentence as you'd say it today."**
   - Write it down verbatim. This is the "before."

2. **"Who are your current clients? Give me 3 names or types."**
   - Are they the clients she *wants*?

3. **"How do clients find you right now?"**
   - Referrals? Google? Architecture firms? Tenders?

4. **"What do you want to change? What's not working?"**
   - Price pressure? Wrong clients? Invisible? Competing on specs?

5. **"If you could only work with ONE type of project for the next 2 years, what would it be?"**
   - This reveals the real ambition.

## BLOCK 2: THE IDEAL CLIENT (10 min)

> Facilitator note: Push past "architects" or "developers." Get specific.

### Questions:

1. **"Describe your best client ever. What made them great?"**
   - Budget? Respect? Project type? Communication style?

2. **"Who is the decision-maker that hires you?"**
   - Architect? Developer? Property owner? Municipality? GC?

3. **"What is that person worried about when they search for a structural engineer?"**
   - Deadlines? Cost overruns? Permits? Innovation? Safety?

4. **"What do they Google? What words do they use?"**
   - "structural engineer [city]"? "timber construction engineer"? Something else?

5. **"What's the first thing they need to believe about you before they call?"**
   - Competence? Specialization? Track record? Modernity?

## BLOCK 3: POSITIONING — WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT (15 min)

> This is the core. Take your time. Push back on generic answers.

### The Positioning Statement Exercise:

Fill in together:

> **We help** [specific client type]
> **who need** [specific outcome/project type]
> **by** [your unique approach/method/advantage]
> **unlike** [typical structural engineering firms who...]
> **because** [proof/credentials/track record].

### Sharpening questions:

1. **"What do you do that other structural engineers don't?"**
   - Specialization? Technology? Speed? Design-forward thinking? Material expertise?

2. **"What's your unfair advantage?"**
   - Patents? Software? Team? Network? Niche expertise?

3. **"If I removed your company name from a project, could someone tell it was your work? Why?"**
   - If no → commodity. If yes → that's the brand.

4. **"Complete this: 'People should come to us when they need ___'"**
   - NOT "structural engineering." Be specific.

5. **"What do your clients say about you that surprises you?"**
   - Often the real positioning lives in client feedback.

### Positioning options to test (pick one direction):

| Positioning Angle | Example |
|-------------------|---------|
| **Specialist** | "The go-to firm for timber & hybrid structures" |
| **Speed/Efficiency** | "Structural engineering that doesn't slow your project down" |

| **Innovation** | "Pushing what's structurally possible" |
| **Trust/Track Record** | "200+ projects. Zero surprises." |
