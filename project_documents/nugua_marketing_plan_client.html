Part 2

The 5 Biggest Problems
Your Future Clients Have

What is this? We researched what construction companies complain about most when working with structural engineers. These are real problems from real companies. The quotes come from industry reports and engineering forums. These are the problems NU GUA can solve.

1

Drawings Are Not Complete

Engineers send drawings that are only 30-75% finished. The construction team has to guess the rest. This causes mistakes and extra costs of 5-10% on the whole project.

"On smaller projects, drawings are about 75% complete. On projects over a million dollars, that number drops to about 30%."

Big Problem

Valeria confirms: Very true. This is a major issue in the industry.

2

Engineers Disappear After Sending Files

The engineer sends the work and is gone. When something goes wrong on the construction site, there is nobody to call. The construction company is alone with the problem.

"Firms send flawed drawings out for bid, and construction teams have to figure out how to make the design work."

Can Happen

Valeria confirms: It happens, but it is not the biggest problem in her area of work.

3

Too Many People, No One Organizes the Information

Big projects have many teams, many rules, many changes. When the engineer cannot manage all this information, everything gets delayed. Delays cost millions.

Big Problem

Valeria confirms: Big problem in her area. She solves it by making clear lists, setting priorities, and sharing documents with all teams.

4

Not Enough Good Professionals in Brazil

Brazil is missing more than 230,000 qualified construction workers. It is the biggest shortage in 20 years. When you are reliable, you become very valuable.

Big Problem

Valeria confirms: Big problem. Reliable professionals are hard to find.

5

Engineers Miss Deadlines

Some engineers want everything perfect, so they never finish on time. They ask for all the data before starting, but miss the deadline. In the public bidding system, a missed deadline means a lost contract.

Medium-High

Valeria confirms: It happens.

Part 3

Our Solution:
O Checklist Estrutural

What is this? Instead of saying "hire us," we give construction companies a free and useful tool: a checklist with 15 questions to ask any structural engineer before signing a contract. When they use this checklist, they will see that most engineers fail. Then they see that NU GUA created it. Then they contact you.

15 Questions Smart Companies Ask
Before Hiring a Structural Engineer

Free PDF, bilingual PT/EN, takes 10 minutes to use

Every question on this list is something you already do well. The checklist is honest and useful. It helps people and makes them trust NU GUA at the same time.

Section 1: Documentation (6 questions)

Do the drawings show all connections and joints?
Do the notes explain the loads, materials, and how things were calculated?
Is there a calculation report you can check?
Are the drawings aligned with the other project teams?
Can the construction team build from these drawings without guessing?
Is there a system to track changes and revisions?

Section 2: Deadlines (5 questions)

Did the engineer give a real timeline before starting?
Will they deliver in parts, or everything at the end?
Do they tell you about delays before it is too late?
When you send late information, do they show you how it affects the schedule?
Do past clients say they met deadlines?

Section 3: How They Work (4 questions)

Do they ask questions about the project, or just accept everything?
Will they join meetings and help make decisions?
Do they take responsibility, or blame whoever gave them the data?
After delivery, can you call them with questions during construction?
