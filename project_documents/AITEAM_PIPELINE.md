## TOP 5 PAIN POINTS

### 1. Incomplete Structural Drawings Causing Costly Rework
**Problem statement:** Construction companies receive structural engineering drawings that are 30-75% complete, leading to confusion on site, rework, and cost overruns of 5-10% of total project value.
**Verbatim quote:** "On smaller projects, drawings are roughly 75% complete. On projects over a million dollars, that number drops to about 30%."
**Source:** Association of the Wall and Ceiling Industry — https://www.awci.org/media/feature-articles/dealing-with-incomplete-drawings/
**Additional source:** Eng-Tips Forum — https://www.eng-tips.com/viewthread.cfm?qid=120794
**Urgency:** High
**Willingness-to-pay signal:** Construction companies already pay premium rates for engineers with proven track records of complete documentation to avoid rework losses.

### 2. Engineers Who Disappear After Delivering Files
**Problem statement:** Structural engineers deliver their calculations and drawings, then vanish. When problems arise on site, the construction company has no engineering partner to solve them.
**Verbatim quote:** "Firms send seriously flawed drawings out for bid, and construction administration has to figure out ways to make the design work."
**Source:** Eng-Tips Forum — https://www.eng-tips.com/viewthread.cfm?qid=120794
**Urgency:** High
**Willingness-to-pay signal:** Construction companies value engineers who "get involved" and "participate in decisions."

### 3. Information Flow Chaos on Complex Infrastructure Projects
**Problem statement:** Infrastructure projects involve dozens of stakeholders, shifting regulatory requirements, and environmental permitting that changes mid-project. Engineers who cannot manage this information flow cause delays that cost millions.
**Verbatim quote:** "Poor communication often leads to incorrect data interpretation, scope confusion. If a team member misreads drawings or specifications, the resulting work may require removal and replacement."
**Source:** Wrench Solutions — https://www.wrenchsp.com/delays-in-construction-projects-due-to-poor-documentation-part-1/
**Urgency:** High
**Willingness-to-pay signal:** R$200K-R$500K project sizes mean even small delays cost significant money.

### 4. Workforce Shortage of 230,000+ Qualified Professionals
**Problem statement:** Brazil's construction sector has the largest workforce deficit in two decades. Construction sites in high-growth regions operating at 60% capacity.
**Verbatim quote:** "The deficit exceeds 230 thousand qualified professionals, the largest in two decades."
**Source:** CPG Click Petróleo e Gás — https://en.clickpetroleoegas.com.br/industria-da-construcao-vive-apagao-de-mao-de-obra-falta-de-pedreiros-mestres-de-obras-e-eletricistas-vml97/
**Urgency:** High
**Willingness-to-pay signal:** Scarcity drives prices up. Reliable partners command premium rates.

### 5. Missed Deadlines from Engineers Who Lose the Big Picture
**Problem statement:** Engineers hyperfocus on technical perfection and demand all parameters before starting, but miss deadlines because they cannot separate what is critical from what is not.
**Verbatim quote:** "Contractor-related causes include... ineffective planning and scheduling issues, poor site management, faulty construction drawings, plan errors and omissions."
**Source:** ScienceDirect — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2090447922001265
**Urgency:** Medium-High
**Willingness-to-pay signal:** In the licitação system, missed deadlines mean lost contracts worth millions.
