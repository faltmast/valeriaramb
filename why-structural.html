The Three Pillars

What we do, why it matters, and what happens without it.

What we do

A structural engineer designs the skeleton of a building — beams, columns, slabs, and foundations. We calculate loads, analyze forces, and determine exact dimensions and materials for every element that holds a structure together. We serve the architect or hydromechanical engineer, taking their project from concept to reality in a way that is safe, economical, and buildable. Our work is the translation layer between design intent and physical reality.

Why it matters

Architecture gives a building its shape. Structural engineering gives it the ability to stand. Without proper structural design, buildings crack, settle unevenly, or fail entirely. Foundations shift under unaccounted loads. Slabs deflect beyond acceptable limits. Walls develop fissures that compromise the entire envelope. Good structural engineering is invisible when done right — you never think about why the building stays up. But when it is done wrong, the consequences are catastrophic. Every beam, every column, every rebar placement exists because an engineer determined it must be there.

What happens without one

Skipping structural engineering leads to cost overruns, unsafe conditions, construction delays, and regulatory failures. Foundations shift because soil conditions were never properly analyzed. Permits get denied because the structural documentation is incomplete or nonexistent. Contractors improvise on site because no one gave them proper detailing. In the worst cases, structures fail — partially or completely. Investing in structural engineering from day one is not an expense. It is insurance. It is the difference between a project that gets built once and one that gets rebuilt three times.

Retrofit & Renovation

Not everything needs to be built from scratch.

Giving spaces new purpose

“My heart is in retrofit.” For Valeria, there is something deeply meaningful about taking an existing space — a building that has already lived a life — and making it work again. Not by demolishing everything and starting over, but by understanding what is there, respecting its bones, and giving it new purpose.

Retrofit is about sustainability and respect for what already exists. It is about analyzing an existing structure, understanding its capacity, identifying what needs reinforcement, and designing solutions that extend its life for decades. A warehouse becomes a cultural center. A deteriorating parking structure gets a second life as mixed-use commercial space. An aging residential building is brought up to modern safety codes without displacing its residents. This work requires a different kind of engineering — one that starts with listening to the structure before telling it what to do.
